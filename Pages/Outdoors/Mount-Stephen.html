Mount Stephen

Right now, this is mostly just a template I'm developing for future posts. Hopefully there's a little something worth seeing, (this is my favourite scramble ever, after all!) but don't get too excited.

I started early-ish (around 8am) at the Parks Canada info centre in Field. My goal was to get the easy part (up past the talus field) done quickly so that I could take my time on the trickier upper part.

The lower part of the mountain was surprisingly steep (someone I ran into in Field claimed the area above the fossil beds was the steepest part, which I disagree with, but just barely). However the routefinding was very easy, which made progress quick. Once I cleared the talus field, I slowed down enormously because finding the easiest way up each ledge took ages.

I ran into another party as I approached the catwalk. They told me to mind the snow and ice that had appeared the night before.

Right before the summit, you have to climb up to a ridge with crazy exposure on either side. It's wide enough to not be dangerous, but narrow enough to provide a thrill.

After much hardship, I reached the summit. Note the small building in the background.

The descent proved easier than I had feared, and I returned to my van with daylight to spare.
